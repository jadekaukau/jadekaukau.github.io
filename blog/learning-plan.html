If I am stuck for longer than 30mins, I need to share via discord and also take a break. Meditate for a few minutes if needed to keep my stress levels down.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

Strengths: Natural problem solver, I will seek the answer through research, try and fail, etc. I also love to learn, so it doesn’t take much motivation for me to start exploring something new.

Limitations: I tend to take on many projects and learn things at the same time, which causes burnout. I also prioritize my social life over learning, but this is slowly changing.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Asking questions. I am never afraid to ask questions, but I find they just don’t pop into my mind when someone says “any questions.” I process on my own and at my own pace. This is from years of studying on my own (and via distance).

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this program.

Each weekday, I commit to at least 1 hour after work hours of study. This usually becomes much longer; however, if my house isn’t in order, I will do washing, make dinner, give my cat some attention, etc. My weekends are dedicated to study unless I have a prebooked engagement, which means I adjust the weekday study sessions to make sure I have plenty of time to complete the work. When things are building up, that’s when I need to cancel social plans and prioritize, calling on my partner for support (they can take care of day-to-day life things). I also divide my study requirements into clear time slots, making sure I stick to them in order to complete the work on time.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

- Stay up to date on study tasks and ask for help.

- If something isn’t working, take a break, then review the work.

- Take advantage of Google to fix a problem.
